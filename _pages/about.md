Dr. Jintao Ke is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Civil Engineering at The University of Hong Kong. He received his B.S. degree in Civil Engineering from Zhejiang University in 2016 and his Ph.D. degree in Civil and Environmental Engineering from The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology in 2020. Prior to joining HKU, he was a Research Assistant Professor at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University.

His research interests include on-demand mobility services, transportation big data analytics, multimodal transportation system optimization, transportation pricing, and spatiotemporal traffic prediction. His research develops models, algorithms, and data-driven quantitative analyses to better manage, operate, and regulate emerging mobility services.

Dr. Ke has published more than 50 SCI/SSCI-indexed research papers in leading journals in transportation research and data mining, including Transportation Research Part A-F, IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems, IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering, IEEE Internet of Things, and Computer-Aided Civil and Infrastructure Engineering. He has been ranked among the World's Top 2% most-cited scientists by Stanford University since 2023 and received the Honorable Mention of HKSTS Outstanding Dissertation Award in 2020.

He serves as an Editorial Board Member of Transportation Research Part C, Transportation Research Part E, and Travel Behavior and Society.

Research Interests
======

- Urban computing and smart cities
- Machine learning and data mining for intelligent transportation systems
- Spatiotemporal traffic pattern analysis and prediction
- Transport economics
- Smart mobility on-demand, including ride-sharing, ride-sourcing, and last-mile delivery
- Land-use and transportation
