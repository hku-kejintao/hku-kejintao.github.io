About

Biography

Dr. Jintao Ke is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Civil Engineering at The University of Hong Kong. He received his B.S. degree in Civil Engineering from Zhejiang University in 2016 and his Ph.D. degree in Civil and Environmental Engineering from The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology in 2020. Before joining HKU, he was a Research Assistant Professor at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University.

His research develops models, algorithms, and data-driven quantitative analyses for managing, operating, and regulating emerging mobility services. He has published more than 50 SCI/SSCI-indexed papers in leading transportation and data mining journals, including Transportation Research Part A-F, IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems, IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering, IEEE Internet of Things, and Computer-Aided Civil and Infrastructure Engineering.

Dr. Ke has been ranked among the World's Top 2% most-cited scientists by Stanford University since 2023 and received the Honorable Mention of the HKSTS Outstanding Dissertation Award in 2020. He serves as an Editorial Board Member of Transportation Research Part C, Transportation Research Part E, and Travel Behavior and Society.
